President Hosni Mubarak and President Teodoro Nguema , the President of the Republic of Equatorial Guinea , agreed to establish resident diplomatic relations in the capitals of the two countries , Cairo and Malabo , in order to support the common desire for strong relations in the areas of investment , commerce and the economy .

Shadi Shash 's report .
President Mohammad Hosni Mubarak and the President of Guinea , Teodoro Obiang Nguema , held a session of talks at the presidential headquarters in Heliopolis where official welcoming ceremonies were held for the Guinean president , the national anthems of both Egypt and Equatorial Guinea were played , and the Honor Guard was reviewed .
Following the welcoming ceremonies , the Guinean president shook hands with those receiving him , led by Dr. AhmedNazif , Prime Minister of the Cabinet , and a number of ministers and senior statesmen .
After the talks , Ambassador Suleiman Awwad , official spokesman for the presidency of the republic , said that the two Presidents Mubarak and Nguema focused in their closed and expanded talks on the overall situation in the African arena , on strengthening relations , and discussed the agenda of the next African summit .
Regarding strengthening bilateral cooperation between Egypt and Equatorial Guinea , Awwad said that Guinea has enormous reserves of oil and gas and comes in third in Africa , and will attain first place in the continent by the year 2010 .
For this reason , Equatorial Guinea is now experiencing vigorous activity in the construction of its infrastructure .
